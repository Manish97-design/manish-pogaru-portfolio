My Journey

Seven years ago, I started my career at Amazon working on Alexa. Today, I help build healthcare compliance software for UK community pharmacies. The path between those two points taught me more about product ownership than any certification ever could.

Making Alexa Work for India

India does not speak one language. It speaks hundreds. At Amazon, my proudest work was "Voice for All," the initiative to bring Alexa to the Indian market across 18 regional languages. Each one came with its own dialects and cultural context. My job was to figure out what users actually needed and ensure the team worked on the right things. By focusing on voice recognition quality, we cut recognition errors by 30% and grew the user base by 1.5 million.

Learning the Business from the Front Line

After my Master's at the University of East Anglia, I took a customer-facing operations job at Travel Chapter to understand the booking ecosystem from the ground up—every complaint and friction point dashboards wouldn't show. That domain knowledge paid off when I moved into Technical Product Owner, where I led the HomeToGo integration in week one and generated £5M in incremental revenue. The best decisions came from proximity to the customer.

Building From Zero in Healthcare
